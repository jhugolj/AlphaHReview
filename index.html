AlphaHReview Methodology

Systematic Literature Review Methodology

This methodology¹ was developed with the aim of prioritizing publication selection criteria and exposing alternatives to improve the systematic literature review method. The AHP tool is used as a methodology to classify the quality criteria of the most relevant publications for the researched topic. Furthermore, the Weitendorf ratios was used to consider and classify the most relevant publications for the researched topic.

STEP 1 - Define the Criteria

Type the names of the criteria to be used in your search in the text boxes.

(You can use 3 to 5 criteria).

STEP 2 - Pairwise Comparison

Using the AHP methodology, perform a pairwise comparison between the selected criteria.


2.1 - (Columns 1 and 2): Compare each pair of elements and provide an ordinal preference judgment (preference dominance);
2.2 - (Columns 3 and 4): Define the weights of the priority criteria*.

*AHP Scale: 1 - Equal Importance, 3 - Moderate Importance, 5 - Strong Importance, 7 - Very Strong Importance, 9 - Extreme Importance, (2, 4, 6 and 8 values in-between).
